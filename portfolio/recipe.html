Pizzelle Recipe

By Calrton Murray

History of the Pizzelle

The pizzelle originates from Italy.
It was created in the 8th century in Abruzzo.
It is commonly made and eaten around christmas time.
The pizzelle is a treat that is usually made and eaten on celebratory days such as weddings or holidays.
A very long time ago families in Italy used to get pizzelle makers that had special celebratory symbols.

About the recipe

The recipe should create a thin light cookie that might look simlar to a waffle.
It should take somewhere around 50 minutes to make.
Before you start you should know that a pizzelle iron is required to make a pizzelle.
Once you finish you should have something that looks similar to this.


Ingredients

  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 cup white sugar
  • 1/2 cup melted butter
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 2 teaspoons vegetable oil, or as needed
  • Pizzelle iron is needed

Directions

  1. Making the batter
    • Beat eggs and sugar in a large bowl until thick. Stir in melted butter and vanilla.
    • Sift together flour and baking powder, and blend into the batter until smooth.
  2. Cooking the pizzelles
    • Heat the pizzelle iron, and brush with oil.
    • Drop about one tablespoon of batter onto each circle on the iron.
    • You may need to experiment with the amount of batter and baking time depending on the iron.
    • Bake for 20 to 45 seconds or until steam is no longer coming out of the iron.
    • Carefully remove cookie from the iron and repeat with remaining batter.
  3. Cooling the pizzelles
    • Cool completely before storing in an airtight container.
